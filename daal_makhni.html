Daal Makhni

Description : Daal Makhni is a cherished Indian lentil dish known for its creamy texture and robust flavors. Slow-cooked black lentils (urad dal) and red kidney beans (rajma) are simmered with aromatic spices, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic, creating a hearty and wholesome dish. Finished with a dollop of butter or cream, Daal Makhni offers a comforting blend of earthy lentils and aromatic spices, making it a staple in Punjabi cuisine and a popular choice for vegetarian meals.


Ingredients

  • Lentil Mixture :
  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak lentils and beans: Soak the black lentils and kidney beans in water overnight.
  2. Cook lentils and beans: Drain and rinse. Pressure cook with 4 cups of water, salt, and turmeric until soft.
  3. Prepare tempering: In a large pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add aromatics: Add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until golden brown.
  5. Add tomatoes: Stir in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  6. Add spices: Mix in red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes.
  7. Combine: Add the cooked lentils and beans to the pan. Simmer on low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add cream: Stir in fresh cream and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  9. Serve: Garnish with a dollop of butter and fresh cream. Serve hot with naan or rice.
