Rasmalai

Description : Rasmalai is a delicate Indian dessert that captivates with its creamy, milky sweetness. Soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) dumplings are soaked in a sweetened and flavored milk syrup infused with cardamom, saffron, and chopped nuts. Chilled and served cold, Rasmalai offers a refreshing contrast to spicy Indian meals and is a delightful conclusion to any feast or celebration, embodying the essence of traditional Indian sweets with every bite.


Ingredients

  • Paneer Balls:
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • Ras (Syrup):
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 10-12 saffron strands
  • 2 tbsp chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)

Instructions

  1. Make paneer: Boil milk in a pot. Add lemon juice to curdle the milk. Drain the curds in a cheesecloth, rinse with cold water, and hang to drain excess water.
  2. Knead paneer: Knead the paneer until smooth and soft. Divide into small balls and flatten slightly.
  3. Cook paneer balls: In a large pot, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil and add the paneer balls. Cook for 15 minutes, then let them cool in the syrup.
  4. Make ras: In another pot, boil milk and reduce to half. Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron strands, and chopped nuts. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Combine: Squeeze paneer balls to remove excess syrup and add them to the milk mixture. Let it soak for at least 2 hours.
  6. Serve: Chill in the refrigerator and serve cold, garnished with more nuts if desired.
